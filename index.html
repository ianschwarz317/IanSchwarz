About

Translating real-world problems
into technology that works

I've spent my career at the front lines of hospitality — close enough to guests and operators to understand where the real friction lives. Most technology in travel is built by people who have never checked a guest in, managed a property during peak season, or navigated the complexity of cross-border payments in real time.

My work sits at the intersection of deep operational experience and a genuine technical skillset. I don't just identify problems — I build the systems to fix them, from architecture to deployment to go-to-market.

The common thread across everything I build: reduce the steps between intent and outcome. Whether that's a hotel guest trying to book directly, a traveler exploring Maine, a business trying to convert a WhatsApp conversation, or a professional trying to save a LinkedIn post — the goal is always the same.
